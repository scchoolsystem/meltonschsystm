Key definitions

  • "School Data" — all data submitted to the Platform by the School, including student records, staff records, financial records, and institutional data.
  • "Users" — administrators, teachers, staff, parents, and students authorised by the School to access the Platform.
  • "Subscription" — a paid licence to access the Platform under a selected plan (Starter, Standard, or Enterprise).
  • "Confidential Information" — any non-public information disclosed by either party in connection with the Platform.

1.2 Access & Account Management

Access to the SmartDev Platform is granted on a per-institution basis. Each school receives a dedicated subdomain (e.g., yourschool.smartdev.co.ke) with complete data isolation from other institutions on the Platform.

The School is responsible for:

  • Maintaining the security and confidentiality of all login credentials issued to its Users.
  • Ensuring that all Users are made aware of and comply with these Terms.
  • Promptly notifying SmartDev of any unauthorised access or suspected security breach.
  • Revoking access for Users who leave the institution or no longer require system access.
  • Ensuring all Users are appropriate for their assigned role (e.g., students only see the student portal).

1.3 Permitted Use

The SmartDev Platform is licensed solely for the purpose of school administration and management by registered Kenyan and East African educational institutions. Permitted uses include:

  • Student enrolment, admissions, and lifecycle management.
  • Academic management — timetables, subjects, marks, exams, report cards.
  • Financial management — fee billing, invoicing, receipt generation, M-Pesa payment processing.
  • Staff HR records, attendance, and departmental management.
  • Boarding, transport, clinic, and welfare management.
  • Parent and student communication via the integrated portals.
  • Library, inventory, kitchen, and asset management.
  • Report generation and institutional analytics.

1.4 Prohibited Actions

The following actions are strictly prohibited and may result in immediate suspension of access without refund:

  • Attempting to reverse engineer, decompile, or extract the source code of the Platform.
  • Using the Platform to process data on behalf of institutions other than the registered School without explicit written permission from SmartDev.
  • Reselling, sublicensing, or otherwise transferring access to the Platform to third parties.
  • Uploading malicious code, viruses, or scripts designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorised access.
  • Attempting to access data belonging to other schools on the Platform.
  • Using automated bots, scrapers, or scripts to extract data in bulk without authorisation.
  • Circumventing or disabling any security features, authentication mechanisms, or access controls.
  • Using the Platform for any purpose that violates Kenyan law, including the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act 2018.
  • Processing data for any purpose other than legitimate educational administration.

1.5 School Content & Data Ownership

All School Data uploaded to or created within the Platform remains the exclusive property of the School. SmartDev claims no ownership over School Data and processes it solely as a data processor acting on the School's instructions.

SmartDev will not access, use, or disclose School Data except:

  • As necessary to provide the Platform services.
  • As directed by the School's authorised administrators.
  • As required by applicable law or regulatory authority.
  • To protect the security and integrity of the Platform.

Upon termination of the Subscription, Schools may export all their data in standard formats (CSV, PDF). Data will be retained for 90 days post-termination and then permanently deleted, unless an extended retention arrangement is agreed in writing.

1.7 Fees & Payment

Subscription fees are as published on the SmartDev pricing page and confirmed in your service agreement. All fees are in Kenyan Shillings (KES) unless otherwise agreed. Fees are payable monthly in advance by bank transfer, M-Pesa, or other methods as agreed.

SmartDev reserves the right to update pricing with 30 days' written notice. Price increases will not apply mid-subscription term for annual subscribers.

Late payment of more than 14 days may result in account suspension. Access will be restored within 24 hours of payment clearing.

1.8 Suspension & Termination

SmartDev may suspend or terminate the School's access to the Platform in the following circumstances:

In the event of termination, the School will be provided with a full export of all School Data in CSV format within 7 business days of the termination date.

2.8 International Data Transfers

School Data is primarily stored in cloud infrastructure located in African or European data centres (Supabase/AWS). Cloudflare's edge network processes request traffic globally. We ensure all cross-border transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards including Standard Contractual Clauses where applicable.

Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

This Data Processing Agreement applies to the processing of personal data by SmartDev on behalf of the School in connection with the SmartDev Platform. It forms part of the Terms of Use and is compliant with the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and GDPR Article 28.

3.2 Controller & Processor Roles

The School acts as the data controller with respect to all School Data. SmartDev acts as a data processor processing data solely on the instructions of the School and for the purposes set out in the Terms of Use and this DPA.

SmartDev processes data in the following capacities for its own purposes (as controller): billing information, platform usage analytics for service improvement, and security logs.

3.3 Processing Instructions

SmartDev processes School Data only on documented instructions from the School. The School's primary instruction is to process data as necessary to deliver the Platform services described in the Terms of Use.

SmartDev will promptly inform the School if, in its opinion, an instruction from the School infringes applicable data protection law.

3.4 Technical & Organisational Safeguards

SmartDev implements and maintains the following measures as a minimum:

  • Pseudonymisation and encryption of personal data where appropriate.
  • Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability and resilience of processing systems.
  • Ability to restore availability and access to personal data in a timely manner following a physical or technical incident.
  • Regular testing and evaluation of technical and organisational measures.

3.5 Sub-processor Management

SmartDev uses the sub-processors listed in Section 9.1. The School provides general authorisation for the use of sub-processors subject to:

  • SmartDev providing at least 14 days' notice before adding or replacing any sub-processor.
  • The School having the right to object to new sub-processors on reasonable data protection grounds.
  • SmartDev imposing equivalent data protection obligations on all sub-processors by written contract.

3.6 Audits & Inspections

SmartDev will make available to the School all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with its obligations under this DPA and allow for audits and inspections conducted by the School or an authorised auditor, with at least 30 days' written notice. SmartDev may charge reasonable fees for audit support beyond one audit per year.

3.7 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach involving School Data, SmartDev will:

  • Notify the School's registered data protection contact without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.
  • Provide: a description of the nature of the breach; the categories and approximate number of data subjects and records affected; likely consequences; and measures taken or proposed.
  • Cooperate with the School in meeting its own notification obligations under the Kenya DPA 2019.
Breach contact

Report data breaches to security@smartdev.co.ke immediately. Our security team monitors this address 24/7.

3.8 Data Subject Access Requests

SmartDev will provide reasonable assistance to the School in responding to data subject access requests, rectification requests, and erasure requests. Requests should be directed to the School, which remains responsible for responding to its own data subjects within the statutory timeframe.

If a data subject contacts SmartDev directly, we will refer them to the relevant School and will not respond to substantive DSAR queries directed at School Data without instruction from the School, except where required by law.

Planned maintenance windows are excluded from the uptime calculation.

  5. Data will be retained for 90 days post-cancellation and then permanently deleted.

If you believe an invoice is incorrect, raise a dispute within 30 days of the invoice date by emailing billing@smartdev.co.ke with full details. Undisputed portions of an invoice remain due on the original payment date. Disputed invoices will be investigated and resolved within 14 business days.

Module 7

Security Policy

7.1 Security Architecture Overview

SmartDev is deployed on Cloudflare Workers with Supabase as the backend database. This architecture provides enterprise-grade security by default:

  • Edge network — all requests pass through Cloudflare's global network with built-in DDoS protection, WAF (Web Application Firewall) and bot mitigation.
  • Multi-tenant isolation — each school is isolated at the database level using Row-Level Security policies. No school can access another school's data.
  • Zero-trust networking — all internal service communication is authenticated and encrypted.
  • Immutable audit logs — all data access and modification events are recorded in tamper-evident audit logs.

7.2 Access Controls

  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with over 20 distinct roles ensures users only access modules relevant to their function.
  • Administrator accounts can enforce multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all staff users.
  • Inactive sessions are automatically terminated after 8 hours.
  • Failed login attempts are rate-limited and trigger alerts after 5 consecutive failures.
  • SmartDev staff access to production systems is restricted to authorised personnel only and requires MFA.

7.4 Vulnerability Management

SmartDev conducts regular security reviews including:

  • Automated dependency vulnerability scanning on every code commit.
  • Annual third-party penetration testing of the production platform.
  • Regular internal security code reviews.
  • Responsible disclosure programme: report vulnerabilities to security@smartdev.co.ke.

7.5 Security Incident Response

Our incident response follows the NIST SP 800-61 framework: Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment → Eradication & Recovery → Post-Incident Activity.

In the event of a confirmed security incident affecting School Data, we will notify the affected School's administrator within 72 hours and provide a full incident report within 14 days of resolution.

Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 — Compliance Statement

SmartDev operates in full compliance with the Kenya Data Protection Act No. 24 of 2019 ("DPA 2019") and the associated Data Protection (General) Regulations 2021. This statement describes how we meet our obligations under the Act.

8.1 Registration with the ODPC

SmartDev, operated by Melton Konchella (Founder & Developer), is registered as a Data Controller and Processor with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) in accordance with Section 17 of the DPA 2019. Our registration number is available upon request.

8.2 Lawful Basis for Processing

We process personal data on the following lawful bases under the DPA 2019:

  • Contractual necessity — processing necessary for the performance of the service agreement with the School (DPA 2019, Section 30(b)).
  • Legal obligation — processing required to comply with applicable Kenyan law (DPA 2019, Section 30(c)).
  • Legitimate interests — processing for security, fraud prevention and service improvement where these interests are not overridden by data subject interests (DPA 2019, Section 30(f)).
  • Consent — where relied upon, Schools are responsible for obtaining and recording valid consent from data subjects.

8.3 Data Subject Rights under DPA 2019

We uphold all rights provided under Part IV of the DPA 2019, including the right of access (Section 26), right to rectification (Section 27), right to deletion (Section 28), right to object (Section 32), and the right to data portability (Section 34).

8.4 ODPC Notification & Complaints

Data subjects who are dissatisfied with our response to a data subject rights request may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner at www.odpc.go.ke or on the ODPC complaint portal.

Module 8.2

GDPR Compliance Statement

While SmartDev primarily serves Kenyan institutions, some of our customers and their data subjects may fall within the scope of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or the UK GDPR. This statement describes our position for such cases.

For EU/UK data subjects, SmartDev relies on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for any personal data transfers outside the EEA/UK. Schools who require EU/UK data processing addenda should contact admin@smartdev.co.ke.

8.3 Children's Data Policy

SmartDev processes personal data about students, who are often under 18. We apply the following additional protections:

  • Schools are the data controllers for student data and are responsible for obtaining valid consent (from parents/guardians for students under 18) before entering student data into the Platform.
  • Student portal access is designed to show students only their own academic and administrative information — nothing outside their personal scope.
  • Health records, disciplinary records, and counselling notes are restricted to staff with explicit need-to-know permissions. Students cannot access these records.
  • Student photos are used only for ID card generation and profile identification within the Platform. They are never used commercially.
  • Data minimisation: we collect only the minimum student data necessary for the educational purposes of each module.
